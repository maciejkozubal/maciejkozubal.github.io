LLMs & Gen AI

Large language models collapsed many NLP subfields into a single interface. Understanding them means understanding transformers, the pretraining/fine-tuning split, and how to evaluate open-ended output.

Core ideas

Transformers as flexible sequence processors · self-attention as a differentiable lookup · pretraining on massive corpora · alignment via supervised fine-tuning and preference optimization.

Key topics

Pretraining vs fine-tuning · RLHF / DPO · Tokenizers & context windows · Parameter-efficient tuning (LoRA, QLoRA) · Evaluation harnesses · Decoding strategies

Learning resources

Andrej Karpathy — "Let's build GPT" seriesvideo · essential The Illustrated Transformer — Jay Alammararticle Hugging Face — NLP coursecourse · free "Attention Is All You Need" — Vaswani et al.paper · 2017 Sebastian Raschka — LLMs from Scratchbook

Things people get wrong

Treating LLMs as oracles instead of as samplers from a conditional distribution. Over-fine-tuning when a prompt change would do. Skipping eval design — "it looks good" is not an eval.
